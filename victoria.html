To use conversation as a design material and develop a novel conversational user interface in the retail store dressing room domain. RETAIL DRESSING ROOMS

understanding the current landscape

Main customer concerns from our research and interviews:

  • Inconvenient to explore additional items (size, color, style), especially when shopping alone
  • Difficulty finding available staff
  • Many customers give up and leave because it's too much hassle to find an associate or the right piece of clothing
  • Most customers shop online now

Main takeaway - most dressing room experiences are terrible and are driving customers away from using them. So how can we improve the experience so that retail stores can bring customers back into the physical store?

NEEDFINDING

identifying areas of opportunity

Based on our competitive analysis, we found that speech-based conversational UIs have yet to be introduced into retail dressing rooms. Existing technologies use graphical UIs that display text and images, such as interactive smart mirrors and e-commerce chatbots.

So why focus on improving the dressing room experience at all?

  • If a customer goes into a dressing room, there is a 2 out of 3 chance she/he will make a purchase.
  • The dressing room is an important touchpoint where human interaction can greatly improve the customer shopping experience.
  • When customers try something on and have a pleasant experience, it increases the likelihood that they will buy the item 3 to 4 times.

Some CUI design issues and challenges to be taken into consideration include:

  • Speech is transient so users must hold all information in working memory.
  • Identifying & repairing errors is difficult & time-consuming.
  • Accuracy of system comprehending user & vice versa.
  • Difficulty of system determining when user needs help.

Despite these challenges, introducing a novel way that customers can interact with the dressing room in physical stores can significantly improve the shopping experience, which could lead to an increase in sales and retention rate. CUIs can enhance the experience by providing customers a more personalized and unique. The goal is to bring customers back into the physical store, and unify their online and in-store shopping experience.

REFRAMING THE PROBLEM

taking a different approach

Instead of trying to design yet another application that focuses on optimizing the scheduling process between home service providers and customers, we decided to approach the problem from a different angle, focusing on building trust between customers and home service providers so that we can eliminate scheduling and rescheduling problems altogether.

VISIONING

user scenarios & storyboards

To explore as many wild ideas as possible, we generated a variety of user scenarios that focuses on building the trust between home service providers and customers. We selected a final scenario and created storyboards to show how value is co-created.

Value for Scott, the Customer
Scott is able to take on an extra substitute job despite the unexpected heater issue. Our solution frees him from the burden of having to wait around at home for a technician, which allows him to keep his schedule open and do the things that he actually wants.

Value for Alyssa, the Home Service Provider/Technician
Our application makes the employees’ jobs significantly more efficient because they no longer need to deal with customers rescheduling or cancelling appointments, or not being home at the scheduled time. They also no longer have to worry about running late to appointments.

Added Value for Alyssa, the Home Service Provider
The recorded videos may be used to train multiple new employees at once instead of having ridealongs which cost more time and money.

CONCEPT REFINEMENT

pitch presentation feedback

We received the following feedback based on our first pitch:

  • Does this really enforce trust or does it make the technicians feel monitored and micromanaged?
  • Does the progress bar enforce trust?
  • How is the phone mounted on? What if the technician's hands are dirty?
  • How can the technician enter the home?

The following changes were made in response:

  • Reframed the livestreaming as a showcase that can provide evidence of the technician's skills and the work they do during the repair. This can also help justify any large bills.
  • Removed the progress bar as its added benefits were not aligned with our goals.
  • Emphasized that the livestream puts all parties (customer, technician and home service provider) at ease for unsupervised services.
  • Researched and recommended possible hardware that home service providers can buy to facilitate mounting a phone for hands-free recording.
