o v e r v i e w

Victoria is Burberry's Conversational Style Expert that is designed to provide loyalty members a highly customized and novel experience in the physical dressing room. The goal is to increase store revenue and customer retention by enhancing the in-store shopping experience by providing expert style advice and personalized product recommendations. Victoria is refined, knowledgeable, and has a dry sense of humor. She can help you coordinate outfits for a special occasion, as well as provide you feedback on the items you try on based on your personal features - your skin, hair, and eye colors.

t h e    c h a l l e n g e

To use conversation as a design material and develop a novel conversational user interface in the retail store dressing room domain. Consider the match between the service goal and and customer needs to design the interactive and functional forms of the CUI, while balancing user freedom/expressivity and system error handling. The CUI must provide good feedforward and feedback, and CANNOT involve phone or in-hand device interactions.

RETAIL DRESSING ROOMS

understanding the current landscape

Main customer concerns from our research and interviews:

  • Inconvenient to explore additional items (size, color, style), especially when shopping alone
  • Difficulty finding available staff
  • Many customers give up and leave because it's too much hassle to find an associate or the right piece of clothing
  • Most customers shop online now

Main takeaway - most dressing room experiences are terrible and are driving customers away from using them. So how can we improve the experience so that retail stores can bring customers back into the physical store?

NEEDFINDING

identifying areas of opportunity

Based on our competitive analysis, we found that speech-based conversational UIs have yet to be introduced into retail dressing rooms. Existing technologies use graphical UIs that display text and images, such as interactive smart mirrors and e-commerce chatbots.

So why focus on improving the dressing room experience at all?

  • If a customer goes into a dressing room, there is a 2 out of 3 chance she/he will make a purchase.
  • The dressing room is an important touchpoint where human interaction can greatly improve the customer shopping experience.
  • When customers try something on and have a pleasant experience, it increases the likelihood that they will buy the item 3 to 4 times.

Some CUI design issues and challenges to be taken into consideration include:

  • Speech is transient so users must hold all information in working memory.
  • Identifying & repairing errors is difficult & time-consuming.
  • Accuracy of system comprehending user & vice versa.
  • Difficulty of system determining when user needs help.

Despite these challenges, introducing a novel way that customers can interact with the dressing room in physical stores can significantly improve the shopping experience, which could lead to an increase in sales and retention rate. CUIs can enhance the experience by providing customers a more personalized and unique. The goal is to bring customers back into the physical store, and unify their online and in-store shopping experience.

ITERATION 1

creating a novel experience for customers

Target Store: middle-end stores with clothing for a variety of occasions
Target User: lone shoppers who would like advice from a friend

System Description

  • Facial recognition camera captures facial features (skin tone, hair color, eye color)
  • Outfit coordination - what matches; complementary items (styles, color, etc.); recommendations by occasion
  • Personalized style feedback - opinion of how items look on you (based on your personal features)
  • Loyalty members scan QR code so the system can analyze past purchases to provide better recommendations
  • Helps shoppers who are self-conscious or uncomfortable with asking a store associate for help.

f i n a l    s o l u t i o n

Target Store: Burberry
Target User: Customers who are members of Burberry's loyalty program, shopping alone

CUI Personality: British, female, air of higher social class - royal, aristocratic, high social status

System Description

  • Customer’s online profile allows him/her to upload a photo from which skin, hair & eye color are automatically extracted, or can be manually inputted. This information is used along with the customer’s purchase history to provide personalized product recommendations.
  • Outfit coordination - what matches; complementary items (styles, color, etc.); recommendations by occassion
  • Personalized style feedback - opinion of how items look on you (based on your personal features)
  • Members-only experience
  • If no color specified, system assigns a higher priority to items that match the customer’s skin, eye, and/or hair color
  • System captures minimum and maximum price range
  • Overlays the top algorithm-recommended item on the smart mirror.
  • Change rate of speech and amplitude depending on speech from user
