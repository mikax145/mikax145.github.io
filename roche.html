This is a 7-month long end-to-end client project with Roche Molecular Diagnostics. Our project is divided into two main halves: 1) the first half was focused on secondary and primary research, as well as some early prototypes, and 2) the second half, which we are currently in, have been for further ideation and iterative cycles of prototyping and user testing.

Team Panacea was given the enormous task to understand the medical oncologist's current ecosystem, workflows, and challenges to identify areas of opportunity that could aid the oncologist's work and patient care. Our team was basically thrown into a sea of the unknown, especially since none of us were familiar with the health industry.

IDENTIFYING OPPORTUNITY AREAS

interview as many users to understand current workflows & painpoints

For the first 3.5 months, we conducted extensive secondary and primary research on the oncology health care team. After synthesizing our initial findings, we identified 3 main areas of opportunity that we could pursue. To further narrow down our focus and validate these opportunity areas, we conducted interviews and shadowed oncologists to get a more accurate understanding of the entire ecosystem.

prioritize user needs to guide product direction
  • Medical oncologists (MOs) struggle to get a holistic view of their patients since the Electronic Medical Records (EMR) stores all data in plain text.
  • Information in the EMR is nested - it's extremely difficult and time-consuming to find relevant information.
  • MOs currently have no way of tracking patient side effects in real time in between visits. It's currently only tracked via paper checklist or verbally during visits.
  • Patient report is inaccurate and/or incomplete.
  • MOs and nurses have to constantly repeat information to patients.

v i s i o n i n g

I led a co-visioning session with our client after presenting our research findings to prioritize our ideas and decide on a direction that provides high value for both medical oncologists and Roche.

p r o t o t y p i n g

Team Panacea dove into a cycle of rapid iterations, going back and forth between print/digital and ideating/prototyping. Our goals are to:

  • Surface relevant information that MOs use to determine whether to continue or change a patient's treatment.
  • Provide MOs with a holistic, longitudinal view of their patients.
  • Fill in missing pieces for MOs by collecting real time patient side effect information between visits and visualizing it (hot new ASCO study showing that web-based symptom tracking extends patient life by 5 months)
  • Collect data about MOs treatment decision rationale.
