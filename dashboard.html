PROBLEM SELECTION

identifying main issues in Canada

After doing some background research on Canada's climate and biodiversity threats, my team decided to focus on the issue of oil sand extraction because it is actionable and its impact is still ambiguous. There are significant petroleum deposits in the sand in Alberta (province in Canada), and the extraction for these oils present significant environmental issues to the country (e.g., air and water contamination, negative affects on animals and plants). While many see oil sand extraction as a newly tapped resource, it's important to monitor these efforts carefully to prevent any irreversable damage.

DASHBOARD ITERATIONS

t h e    s o l u t i o n

We designed a dashboard that would present the climate change and biodiversity scientists' data to politicians with the power to implement positive change. Our dashboard allows politicians to monitor environmental factors affected by oil sand extraction, highlight critical situations, and reveal predictive trends. The politicians are incentivized to use this dashboard because it will allow them to make economic decisions and guide Canada through the process of transitioning out of oil sand extraction as it heads toward depletion. Looking to that transition period now could prevent economic recession - measures could be taken ahead of time to stop heavily depending on crude oil exports for revenue and reallocate jobs in the mining sector.
