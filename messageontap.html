This project is an independent study that I worked on with a PhD student under the supervision of Professor Jason Hong at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University. MessageOnTap is an intelligent agent that facilitates efficient instant messaging on smartphones by providing meaningful auto-responses and fast responses to simplify the work that a person would normally do (e.g., don't have to switch between address book and messaging app to find a contact's phone number). The system makes use of different natural language processing techniques and machine learning to read messages and create fast action inputs based on personal data. It is currently implemented as a background Android service and works with WhatsApp and WeChat messenger apps.

how can we further enhance instant messaging communication using contextual intelligence?

In our current digital world, we are constantly connected with each other through the internet and instant messaging has become part of our daily lives. Messaging apps have evolved over the last years to enrich our experience by allowing us to share pictures, videos, locations, emojis, and more with each other. Taking this a step further, services like Facebook messenger and Google Allo can now suggest quick responses based on your conversations, search for images on the internet, and suggest nearby restaurants.

My main tasks were to redesign the existing user interface for suggestive information and design the interaction and experience for various new user scenarios using fast action inputs.
